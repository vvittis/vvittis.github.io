ABOUT ME

I am a graduate student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department of Technical University of Crete. I received my diploma under the supervision of Antonios Deligiannakis.

I am passionate about streaming distributed systems and theoretical machine learning algorithms. The last couple of years, I am working mostly on databases, streaming engines, ensemble learning, concept drift adaptation and graph theory. For more information you can take a look in my CV.

My current research goals are: distributed machine learning algorithms, online neural-networks and deep learning. I want to work in projects where innovative ideas can be proposed in already robust algorithmic solutions and have some real-world applications. Also, I am looking to improve my thesis by making an efficient dynamic-size ensemble.

I am confident about my work ethic and my strong academical background in the field of computer science. My overall GPA is 8.3 and my major GPA is 8.76.

I am first and foremost a problem solver, and search widely to find problems where mathematics (simple or complicated) can answer scientific questions.
